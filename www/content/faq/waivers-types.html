Waiver Wire Formats

The waiver wire, or waivers, is where unclaimed players are put on a temporary hold before teams have an opportunity to add them to a their roster via a claim. Depending on the format, when waivers are processed, the team with the highest waiver priority will be awarded the player. The league commissioner defines the waiver format or type in the league settings. Waiver types include rolling waivers, reverse standings, and FAAB bidding.

There are three main types of waiver wire formats: rolling waivers, reverse standings, and FAAB bidding. Each format has its own advantages and disadvantages.

Rolling Waivers

Rolling waivers is the default format on the Sleeper platform. When a league uses rolling waivers, the waiver priority is continuous, based on the last team to waiver a player being moved to the end of the queue. For every waiver claim that is successful, the team is moved to the end of the queue that adds the player off waivers, and the remaining teams are moved up.

Rolling waivers is a continuous waiver format where the team that last successfully claimed a player is moved to the end of the queue.
Reverse standings waivers prioritize teams with the worst records to help improve their roster.

FAAB Bidding

FAAB, or Free Agent Acquisition Budget, is an alternative to traditional waivers. Each team is provided with a budget (e.g., $100) to acquire players through waivers using a blind bidding process.

FAAB bidding allows teams to acquire players through waivers using a blind bidding process. This gives everyone an opportunity to acquire the players they prefer, rather than relying on waiver priority.

When waivers are processed, the team with the highest bid is awarded the player. Bids can be defined by the commissioner with a required minimum bid, which is set to $0 by default.
